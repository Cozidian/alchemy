Kamala Devi Harris, born on October 20, 1964, in Oakland, California, is an American politician and attorney who served as the 49th Vice President of the United States from 2021 to 2025. She holds the distinction of being the first woman, as well as the first African American and Asian American, to hold the office of Vice President.

Early Life and Education

Harris is the daughter of immigrants: her mother, Shyamala Gopalan, was a biologist from India, and her father, Donald J. Harris, is an economist from Jamaica. She earned a Bachelor of Arts in political science and economics from Howard University in 1986 and a law degree from the University of California, Hastings College of the Law, in 1989.

Legal and Political Career

Harris began her legal career as a deputy district attorney in Alameda County, California. She was elected as the District Attorney of San Francisco in 2003 and later served as the Attorney General of California from 2011 to 2017. In 2016, she was elected to the U.S. Senate, representing California.

Vice Presidency and 2024 Presidential Election

In 2020, Harris was selected as Joe Biden's running mate, and they won the presidential election. Following President Biden's decision not to seek re-election in 2024, Harris became the Democratic Party's nominee for president. She was defeated by former President Donald Trump in the 2024 election.

Personal Life

Harris is married to attorney Douglas Emhoff, and she is a stepmother to his two children. Throughout her career, she has been recognized for her advocacy on issues such as criminal justice reform, healthcare, and civil rights.
